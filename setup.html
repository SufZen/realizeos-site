0 Before You Start

Gather these before you begin. Having them ready means you can go from download to a working AI team in one sitting.

  • Business name — the name of your business or venture
  • Brand voice sample — a paragraph you've written that sounds like "you" (LinkedIn post, email, anything)

Save location matters

Save your vault in Documents, not in a cloud-synced folder (OneDrive, iCloud, Google Drive). Cloud sync causes conflicts with Obsidian.

4 Define Your Brand 5 min

This is the most important step

The #1 reason people get generic AI output: they skip or rush the brand definition. 5 minutes here saves hours of frustration later. This single step is the difference between generic AI and your AI.

Brand Identity

  1. Open F-foundations/brand-identity.md
  2. Fill in: business name, mission, target audience, differentiator, values

Brand Voice

  1. Open F-foundations/brand-voice.md
  2. Fill in: personality, tone, vocabulary, dos and don'ts

Key tip: Paste a real paragraph you've written into the "Good Example" section. This gives the AI a concrete reference for how you sound.

Key tip: Add anti-patterns — what you NEVER want to sound like. Examples: "Never use corporate jargon", "Never start with 'In today's fast-paced world'", "Never use exclamation marks." Anti-patterns are surprisingly powerful.

If you completed the Brand Worksheet, paste the exported markdown into these files — most of the work is already done.

What you should see

  • Two markdown files with structured templates
  • Clear guidance on what to write in each section
  • Your specific rules, not placeholder text
  • If you leave these vague ("be professional and helpful"), your output will be generic. Be specific.
  • brand-identity.md filled with your business details
  • brand-voice.md has specific tone rules and examples
  • At least 3 anti-patterns defined (what your AI should NEVER do)

Troubleshooting

Common issues and their fixes. Click any item to expand.

You likely opened the wrong folder. Close the vault, then re-open by selecting the folder that contains the FABRIC subfolders (A-agents/, B-brain/, etc.). If you unzipped into a nested folder, look one level deeper.
Node.js isn't installed, or it's not in your PATH.

Fix 1: Close and reopen your terminal after installing Node.js.
Fix 2: Run node --version to check if Node.js is installed.
Fix 3: On macOS, try brew install node. On Windows, download from nodejs.org and check "Add to PATH" during install.
Your brand-voice.md is too vague. Vague instructions like "be professional" don't help. Instead, write specific rules:

• "Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)"
• "Start with data, not opinions"
• "No exclamation marks"
• "Never use the word 'synergy'"

Also paste a real writing sample into the "Good Example" section.
Make sure you're running claude from the correct directory — the one containing the CLAUDE.md file. Check with ls (Mac/Linux) or dir (Windows) that the file exists in your current working directory.
macOS may block terminal access to your files.

Fix: Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Full Disk Access → Enable your terminal app (Terminal, iTerm2, etc.)
Git isn't installed. Download from git-scm.com. After installing, close and reopen your terminal for the PATH to update.
Windows: Make sure WSL2 is installed. Open PowerShell as admin and run wsl --install. Restart your computer.
Mac: Check you downloaded the right version (Apple Silicon vs Intel).
Both: Make sure Docker Desktop is actually running (check the system tray/menu bar icon). Verify virtualization is enabled in BIOS.
Check your .env file:
• No spaces around the = sign
• No quotes around the value
• No trailing whitespace — re-type the key if needed
• Key might be revoked — create a new one at console.anthropic.com
