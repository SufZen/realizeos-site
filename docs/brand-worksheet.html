Part 1

Who You Are

This is about you — the person behind the business. Your AI team uses this to understand your perspective and how you like to work.

01 What's your name, and what's your role?

E.g., "Sara Cohen, Founder & CEO of Brandly" — the AI will address you and reference your role.

maps to → shared/identity.md > About You

02 What are your top 3 strengths?

What you're best at — the AI should amplify these, not compensate for them.

maps to → shared/identity.md > Your Strengths

03 Where do you need the most help?

Your gaps — where the AI should focus to give you the highest leverage.

maps to → shared/identity.md > Your Gaps

04 How do you like to receive information?

Length, format, and decision style preferences. The AI will adapt to this.

maps to → shared/identity.md > Communication Preferences

Part 2

Your Business

This is about your business — how it should be positioned and what it stands for. Your AI team will represent this to the world.

05 What's your business name and tagline?

The tagline should capture what you do in one memorable sentence.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Business Name & Tagline

06 What's your mission? Why does this business exist?

The problem you solve and the outcome you create. Be specific — not "we make the world better."

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Mission

07 Who is your ideal customer? Describe one specific person.

Role, industry, company size, pain points. Picture a real human, not a segment.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Target Audience

08 What are your 3-5 core values?

Values that guide every decision. Include what each one looks like in practice.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Core Values

09 What makes you different? Finish this sentence:

"We are the only [category] that [unique benefit] for [audience] by [method]."

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Unique Value Proposition

10 Where do you sit in the market?

We ARE: [what you stand for]. We are NOT: [what you're not]. Be honest and specific.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Competitive Positioning

11 What are your main products or services?

List 2-4 key offerings with a one-line description each.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Key Offerings

Part 3

Your Voice

The difference between AI that sounds like you and AI that sounds like everyone else. This is the most impactful section.

12 If your brand was a person, how would you describe them?

Primary tone, secondary tone, and a "sounds like" analogy. E.g., "A trusted advisor who explains complex things simply."

maps to → F-foundations/brand-voice.md > Tone of Voice

13 Words you use. Words you avoid.

5+ words/phrases that feel like your brand, and 3+ words that feel off-brand.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-voice.md > Vocabulary

14 What are your content formatting preferences?

Sentence length, paragraph length, use of lists, emoji usage, capitalization style.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-voice.md > Formatting Rules

15 Your do's and don'ts

3-4 rules you always follow, and 3-4 things you never do.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-voice.md > Do's and Don'ts

16 Write a paragraph that sounds exactly like you

A real example from your content, or write one now. This is the single most powerful input for voice calibration.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-voice.md > Voice Examples > Good Example

17 Write a paragraph that sounds nothing like you

The counter-example. Useful for the AI to understand what to avoid.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-voice.md > Voice Examples > Bad Example

18 What 3 workflows do you repeat every week?

Content writing? Client proposals? Market research? These become your first AI skills.

maps to → R-routines/ > Skill Definitions
