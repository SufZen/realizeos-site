Part 1

Your Business

The foundation. One sentence that makes people lean in — and a reason they pick you.

01 In one sentence, what does your business do?

What you offer, for whom, and the outcome they get.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Mission

02 What problem do you solve?

What keeps your customers up at night? What are they tired of doing manually?

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Problem

03 Why you, not your competitors?

2-3 things they can't copy. Be specific — not "we care about quality."

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Differentiator

Part 2

Your Audience

Picture one person. Not a segment. A human with a name, a role, and a problem they need solved.

04 Describe your ideal customer

Role, approximate age, industry, company size. Picture one real person.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Target Audience

05 What do they want to achieve?

Their main goal. What would make them feel successful?

maps to → F-foundations/brand-identity.md > Audience Goals

06 What's blocking them?

Their main frustration or obstacle. The thing they've tried to fix but can't.

maps to → B-brain/domain-knowledge.md > Customer Obstacles

Part 3

Your Voice

The difference between AI that sounds like you and AI that sounds like everyone else. This is the most impactful section.

07 If your brand was a person, how would you describe them?

Character traits. E.g., "A sharp friend who cuts through noise and gives you the real answer."

maps to → F-foundations/brand-voice.md > Personality

08 What should your content NEVER sound like?

2-3 anti-patterns. E.g., "Not corporate", "Not salesy", "Never use exclamation marks."

maps to → F-foundations/brand-voice.md > Anti-Patterns

09 Write a sentence that sounds like you

A real sentence from your content, or one you could write. This is the most powerful input for voice calibration.

maps to → F-foundations/brand-voice.md > Voice Example

10 What 3 workflows do you repeat every week?

Content writing? Client proposals? Market research? Meeting prep? Be specific — these become your first AI skills.

maps to → R-routines/ > Skill Definitions
